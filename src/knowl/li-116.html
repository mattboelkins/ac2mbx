Item 2
\(y = g(x)\) such that
  • \(g(-2) = 3\), \(g(-1) = -1\), \(g(1) = -2\), and \(g(2) = 3\)
  • At \(x = -2, -1, 1\) and \(2\), \(g\) has a limit, and its limit equals the value of the function at that point.
  • \(g(0)\) is not defined and \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} g(x)\) does not exist.
