\item We have shown that if \(\sum (-1)^{k+1} a_k\) is a convergent alternating series, then the sum \(S\) of the series lies between any two consecutive partial sums \(S_n\). This suggests that the average \(\frac{S_n+S_{n+1}}{2}\) is a better approximation to \(S\) than is \(S_n\).
