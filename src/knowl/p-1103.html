{ The Total Change Theorem: \knownindex{\lt main>total change theorem\lt /main>} If \(f\) is a continuously differentiable function on \([a,b]\) with derivative \(f'\), then \( f(b) - f(a) = \int_a^b f'(x) \, dx. \) That is, the definite integral of the derivative of a function on \([a,b]\) is the total change of the function itself on \([a,b]\). }
