Observe that Equation (4.3.2) tells us another way to interpret the definite integral: the definite integral of a function \(f\) from \(a\) to \(b\) is the length of the interval \((b-a)\) times the average value of the function on the interval. In addition, Equation (4.3.2) has a natural visual interpretation when the function \(f\) is nonnegative on \([a,b]\).
