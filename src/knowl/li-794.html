Item 4
By the quotient rule and algebraic simplification, \[ I'(t) = \frac{e^t 100 - 100te^t}{(e^t)^2} = \frac{100-100t}{e^t}. \] Thus, \(I'(0.5) = \frac{50}{e^{0.5}} \approx 30.327\), \(I'(2) = \frac{-100}{e^{2}} \approx -13.534\), and \(I'(5) = \frac{-400}{e^4} \approx -2.695\), each measured in candles per millisecond. These results show that at \(t = 0.5\), the intensity of the flash is increasing rapidly, while at \(t = 2\) and \(t = 5\), the intensity is decreasing, with the intensity decreasing more rapidly when \(t = 2\).
