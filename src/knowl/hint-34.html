Hint 1.6.4.6Remember that to plot \(y = f'(x)\), it is helpful to first identify where \(f'(x) = 0\), and then ask where \(y = f'(x)\) is positive and negative. In a similar way, once \(y = f'(x)\) has been plotted, to construct the graph of \(y=f''(x)\), it is useful to note where the slope of the tangent line to \(y = f'(x)\) is zero, as well as where such slopes are positive and negative. Heights on the graph of \(y = f''(x)\) will correspond to slopes on \(y = f'(x)\).in-context
