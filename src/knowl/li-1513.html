Item 3
Since the water in the tank always takes the shape of a circular cone, the volume of water in the tank at time \(t\) is given by \(V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\). Because we have established that \(r = \frac{3}{4}h\), it follows that \[ V = \frac{1}{3}\pi \left( \frac{3}{4}h \right)^2 h = \frac{3}{16} \pi h^3. \]
