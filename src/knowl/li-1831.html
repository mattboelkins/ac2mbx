Item 2
Since \(\frac{d}{dx} [\cos(x)] = -\sin(x)\), an antiderivative of \(f(x) = \sin(x)\) is \(F(x) = -\cos(x)\). Therefore, by the FTC, \[ \ds \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \sin(x) \, dx = \left. -\cos(x) \right|_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}, \] so \[ \ds \int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \sin(x) \, dx = -\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}) - (-\cos(0)) = -0 + 1 = 1. \]
