Solution 8.3.2.1
  1. The \(n\)th partial sum of the series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_k\) is \[ S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n a_k. \]
  2. The \((n-1)\)st partial sum of the series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_k\) is \[ S_{n-1} = \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} a_k. \]
  3. The difference between \(S_n\) and \(S_{n-1}\) is \[ \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} a_k = a_{n}. \]
  4. Since \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_k = \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n\) we must have \(\lim_{n \to \infty} S_n = L\). Also, \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} S_{n-1}, \] so \(\lim_{n \to \infty} S_{n-1} = L\) as well.
  5. We have \begin{align*} \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n \amp = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(S_n - S_{n-1}\right)\\ \amp = \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n - \lim_{n \to \infty} S_{n-1}\\ \amp = L - L\\ \amp = 0. \end{align*}
