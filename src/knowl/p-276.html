It is essential to recall that when \(f\) is differentiable at \(x = a\), the value of \(f'(a)\) provides the slope of the tangent line to \(y = f(x)\) at the point \((a,f(a))\). By knowing both a point on the line and the slope of the line we are thus able to find the equation of the tangent line.
