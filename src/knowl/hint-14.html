Hint 1.3.2.2
  1. Observe that the function \(f\) is of the form \(f(x) = mx + b\).
  2. To compute the average rate of change of \(f\) on \([1,4]\), we calculate \(\frac{f(4)-f(1)}{4-1}\).
  3. Note that \(f(1+h) - f(1) = (3 - 2(1+h)) - (3-2) = 3 - 2 - 2h - 1 = -2h\).
  4. Remember that \(f'(a)\) represents the slope of the function \(f\) at the value \(a\).
