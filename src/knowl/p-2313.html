\item The sequence of partial sums of a convergent alternating series oscillates around and converge to the sum of the series if the sequence of \(n\)th terms converges to 0. That is why the Alternating Series Test shows that the alternating series \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} (-1)^ka_k\) converges whenever the sequence \(\{a_n\}\) of \(n\)th terms decreases to 0. \item The difference between the \(n-1\)st partial sum \(S_{n-1}\) and the \(n\)th partial sum \(S_n\) of a convergent alternating series \(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} (-1)^ka_k\) is \(|S_n - S_{n-1}| = a_n\).
