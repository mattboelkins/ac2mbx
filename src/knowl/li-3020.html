Item 6
Notice that \[ \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{ \ln(k) }{ k^{1/2} } = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{2}{k^{1/2}} = 0, \] So \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{k}}\) dominates \(\ln(k)\) and \(\ln(k) \lt sqrt{k}\) for large \(k\). It follows that \[ \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2} \lt \frac{ \sqrt{k} }{k^2} = \frac{1}{k^{3/2}} \] for large \(k\). Therefore, \[ \sum \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2} \lt \sum \frac{1}{k^{3/2}} \] for large \(k\). Since \(\ds \sum \frac{1}{k^{3/2}}\) is a \(p\)-series with \(p=\frac{3}{2} > 1\), the series \(\ds \sum \frac{1}{k^{3/2}}\) converges. This forces the series \(\sum \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2}\) to converge as well. So \(\ds \sum (-1)^k \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2}\) converges absolutely. \end{thmlist}
