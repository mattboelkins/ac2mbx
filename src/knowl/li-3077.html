Item 1
Find a formula for \(P_1(x)\), the linearization of \(f(x)\) at \(x=0\). (We label this linearization \(P_1\) because it is a first degree polynomial approximation.) Recall that \(P_1(x)\) is a good approximation to \(f(x)\) for values of \(x\) close to \(0\). Plot \(f\) and \(P_1\) near \(x=0\) to illustrate this fact. Solution

We know that

\[ P_1(x) = f(0) + f'(0)x = 1+x. \]

Since \(P_1(0) = f(0) = 1\) and \(P'_1(0) = f'(0) = 1\), the graphs of \(P_1\) and \(f\) agree at \(x=a\) and have the same slope at \(x=0\) (which means they go in the same direction at \(x=0\)). This is why \(P_1(x)\) is a good approximation to \(f(x)\) for values of \(x\) close to \(0\).
