Item 2
Note that \begin{align*} \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{a_k}{b_k} \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{ \frac{k+1}{k^3+2} }{ \frac{k}{k^3} }\\ \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{(k+1)k^3}{k(k^3+2)}\\ \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{1+\frac{1}{k}}{1+\frac{2}{k}}\\ \amp = 1. \end{align*} This tells us that \(a_k \approx b_k\) for large values of \(k\), which is what we suggested in part (a).
