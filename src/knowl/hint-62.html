Hint 2.3.4.2
  1. Observe that \(f\) is fundamentally a product. Which is the first function? The second? What rule(s) are needed to differentiate the first function? The second?
  2. Note that \(p\) has the overall structure of a quotient. What is the structure of the bottom function?
  3. Think about how \(g\) is a sum of three functions. What is the structure of each of the three functions in the sum?
  4. How is the velocity of a moving object related to its position? What is the structure of \(s(t)\)?
  5. Since we know \(p(x) = f(x) \cdot g(x)\), it follows \(p'(x) = f(x) g'(x) + g(x) f'(x)\) for any value of \(x\). How can you use the given information?
