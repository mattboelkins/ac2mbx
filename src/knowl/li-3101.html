Item 8
The first four derivatives of \(f(x)\) at \(x=0\) are \begin{align*} f(x) \amp = \frac{1}{1-x} \amp \hspace{0.5in} \amp f(0) \amp = 1\\ f'(x) \amp = \frac{1}{(1-x)^2} \amp \hspace{0.5in} \amp f'(0) \amp = 1\\ f''(x) \amp = \frac{2}{(1-x)^3} \amp \hspace{0.5in} \amp f''(0) \amp = 2\\ f^{(3)}(x) \amp = \frac{3!}{(1-x)^4} \amp \hspace{0.5in} \amp f^{(3)}(0) \amp = 3!\\ f^{(4)}(x) \amp = \frac{4!}{(1-x)^5} \amp \hspace{0.5in} \amp f^{(4)}(0) \amp = 4!. \end{align*} It appears that the pattern is \[ f^{(k)}(0) = k!. \]
