Item 5
By L'Hopital's Rule we have \[ \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2} = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{1}{2k^2} = 0. \] Also, \[ \frac{d}{dk} \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2} = \frac{k-2kln(k)}{k^4} = \frac{1-2ln(k)}{k^3} \] is negative when \(k > e\), so the sequence \(\left\{ \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2} \right\}\) ultimately decreases to 0. Since the first few terms in a series are irrelevant to its convergence or divergence, we conclude that the series \(\ds \sum (-1)^k \frac{\ln(k)}{k^2}\) converges by the Alternating Series test.
