We use each subinterval \([x_i, x_{i+1}]\) as the base of a rectangle, and next must choose how to decide the height of the rectangle that will be used to approximate the area under \(y = f(x)\) on the subinterval. There are three standard choices: use the left endpoint of each subinterval, the right endpoint of each subinterval, or the midpoint of each. These are precisely the options encountered in Preview Activity  and seen in Figure 4.2.2. We next explore how these choices can be reflected in sigma notation.
