Item 5
Since \(p(x) = f(x) \cdot g(x)\), the product rule tells us \[ p'(x) = f(x)g'(x) + g(x)f'(x), \] and since \(\displaystyle q(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\), by the quotient rule we know \[ q'(x) = \frac{g(x)f'(x)-f(x)g'(x)}{g(x)^2}. \] Using the given information (\(f(3) = -2\), \(f'(3) = 7\), \(g(3) = 4\), and \(g'(3) = -1\)) we now see that \[ p'(3) = f(3)g'(3) + g(3)f'(3) = (-2)(-1) + (4)(7) = 30 \] and \[ q'(x) = \frac{g(3)f'(3)-f(3)g'(3)}{g(3)^2} = \frac{(4)(7) - (-2)(-1)}{4^2} = \frac{13}{8}. \]
