Item 5
Using the sum and constant multiple rules for differentiation, we can see that similar results hold for antidifferentiation, and thus that \(F(x) = \frac{3}{4} x^4 - \frac{2}{3} x^3 - e^x\) is an antiderivative of \(f(x) = 3x^3 - 2x^2 - e^x\). Now, by the FTC, \begin{align*} \int_0^2 (3x^3 - 2x^2 - e^x) \, dx \amp = \amp \left. \frac{3}{4} x^4 - \frac{2}{3} x^3 - e^x \right|_0^2\\ \amp = \amp \frac{3}{4} (2)^4 - \frac{2}{3} (2)^3 - e^2 - (\frac{3}{4} (0)^4 - \frac{2}{3} (0)^3 - e^0)\\ \amp = \amp 12 - \frac{16}{3} - e^2 - (0 - 0 - 1)\\ \amp = \amp \frac{17}{3} - e^2. \end{align*}
