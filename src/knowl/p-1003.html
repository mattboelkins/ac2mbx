We call the symbol \(\int\) the integral sign\knownindex{\lt main>integral sign\lt /main>}, the values \(a\) and \(b\) the limits of integration\knownindex{\lt main>limits of integration\lt /main>}, and the function \(f\) the integrand\knownindex{\lt main>integrand\lt /main>}. The process of determining the real number \(\int_a^b f(x) \, dx\) is called evaluating the definite integral. While we will come to understand that there are several different interpretations of the value of the definite integral, for now the most important is that \(\int_a^b f(x) \, dx\) measures the net signed area bounded by \(y = f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis on the interval \([a,b]\).
