Item 1
We evaluate the improper integral: \begin{align*} \int_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \int_{1}^{t} \frac{1}{x^2} \ dx\\ \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} -\frac{1}{x} \biggm|_{1}^{t}\\ \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \left(-\frac{1}{t} + \frac{1}{1} \right)\\ \amp = 1. \end{align*} So the improper integral converges. The Integral Test then shows that the series \( \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k^2}\) converges.
