Hence, Equation (5.3.1) tells us that if we can take a given function and view its algebraic structure as \(f'(g(x)) g'(x)\) for some appropriate choices of \(f\) and \(g\), then we can antidifferentiate the function by reversing the Chain Rule. It is especially notable that both \(g(x)\) and \(g'(x)\) appear in the form of \(f'(g(x)) g'(x)\); we will sometimes say that we seek to identify a function-derivative pair\knownindex{\lt main>function-derivative pair\lt /main>} when trying to apply the rule in Equation (5.3.1).
