Item 1
Let \(f(x) = \sqrt{e^x + 3}.\) By the chain rule \(f'(x) = \frac{e^x}{2\sqrt{e^x + 3}}\), and thus \(f'(0) = \frac{1}{4}\). Note further that \(f(0) = \sqrt{1 + 3} = 2\). The tangent line is therefore the line through \((0,2)\) with slope \(1/4\), which is \[ y - 2 = \frac{1}{4}(x-0). \]
