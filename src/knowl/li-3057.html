Item 3
Determine the number of terms it would take in the series (8.4.3) to approximate \(\frac{\pi}{4}\) to 10 decimal places. (The fact that it takes such a large number of terms to obtain even a modest degree of accuracy is why we say that conditionally convergent series converge very slowly.) Solution

In this case we have

\[ |S_n - \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{k} \frac{1}{2k+1}| \lt a_{n+1} = \frac{1}{2n+1}. \]

To have \(a_{n+1} \lt 10^{-10}\) would require

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2n+1} \amp \lt 10^{-10}\\ 2n+1 \amp > 10^{10}\\ n \amp > \frac{10^{10}-1}{2}\\ n \amp > 4,999,999,999.5. \end{align*}
