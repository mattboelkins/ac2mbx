At times, the shape of a geometric region may dictate that we need to use horizontal rectangular slices, rather than vertical ones. For instance, consider the region bounded by the parabola \(x = y^2 - 1\) and the line \(y = x-1\), pictured in FigureÂ 6.1.5. First, we observe that by solving the second equation for \(x\) and writing \(x = y + 1\), we can eliminate a variable through substitution and find that \(y+1 = y^2 - 1\), and hence the curves intersect where \(y^2 - y - 2 = 0\). Thus, we find \(y = -1\) or \(y = 2\), so the intersection points of the two curves are \((0,-1)\) and \((3,2)\).
