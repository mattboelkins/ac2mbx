If we say that the total number of rectangles is \(n\), and we desire \(n\) rectangles of equal width to subdivide the interval \([a,b]\), then each rectangle must have width \(\triangle x = \frac{b-a}{n}\). We observe further that \(x_1 = x_0 + \triangle x\), \(x_2 = x_0 + 2 \triangle x\), and thus in general \(x_{i} = a + i\triangle x,\) as pictured in FigureÂ 4.2.4.
