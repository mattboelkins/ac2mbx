Item 3
Letting Since \(f\) is decreasing, the improper integral \(\ds \int_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x} \ dx\) is smaller than the limit of the Riemann sums as \(n\) goes to infinity. So we wind up with a comparison between the series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n}\) and the improper integral: \[ \ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k} > \int_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x} \ dx. \]
