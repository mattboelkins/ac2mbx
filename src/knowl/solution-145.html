Solution .1

The derivatives of \(P_2\) and \(f\) are

\begin{align*} P_2(x) \amp = 1+x+\frac{x^2}{2} \amp f(x) \amp = e^x\\ P'_2(x) \amp = 1 + x \amp f'(x) \amp = e^x\\ P''_2(x) \amp = 1 \amp f''(x) \amp = e^x, \end{align*}

and so the derivatives of \(P_2\) and \(f\) evaluated at 0 are

\begin{align*} P_2(0) \amp = 1 \amp f(0) \amp = e^0 = 1\\ P'_2(0) \amp = 1 \amp f'(0) \amp = e^0 = 1\\ P''_2(0) \amp = 1 \amp f''(0) \amp = e^0 = 1. \end{align*}
