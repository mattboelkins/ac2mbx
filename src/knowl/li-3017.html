Item 3
Note that \begin{align*} \lim_{t \to \infty} \int_{1}^{t} \frac{\ln(x}{x} \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \left. \frac{\ln(x)^2}{2} \right|_1^t\\ \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \frac{\ln(t)^2}{2}\\ \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \frac{\ln(t)^2}{2}\\ \amp = \infty. \end{align*} Since the improper integral diverges, the Integral Test shows that the series \(\ds \sum (-1)^k \frac{\ln(k)}{k}\) diverges. So the series \(\ds \sum (-1)^k \frac{\ln(k)}{k}\) converges conditionally. \end{thmlist}
