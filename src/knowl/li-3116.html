Item 9
Determine the \(n\) order Taylor polynomial for \(f(x) = \frac{1}{1-x}\) centered at \(x=0\). The \(n\)th order Taylor polynomial for \(f\) at \(x=0\) is \[ \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{f^{(k)}}{k!} x^k = \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{k!}{k!} x^k = \sum_{k=0}^n x^k. \] This makes sense since \(f(x)\) is the sum of the geometric series with ratio \(x\), so the \(n\)th order Taylor polynomial should just be the \(n\)th partial sum of this geometric series. \end{thmlist}
