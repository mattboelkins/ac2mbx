Hint 2.4.2.2
  1. Remember that one value of \(x\) for which \(\cos(x) = 0\) is \(x = \frac{\pi}{2}\). What are the others?
  2. Don't forget that \(\frac{d}{dx}[1] = 0\), and be careful with your signs.
  3. Consider rewriting \(\frac{\sin(x)}{\cos^2(x)}\) as \(\frac{1}{\cos(x)} \cdot \frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}\).
  4. Observe that \(\cos(x)\) is still present in the denominator of \(h'(x)\).
