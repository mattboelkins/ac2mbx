Item 3
At sea level, air pressure is 30 inches of mercury. At an altitude of \(h\) feet above sea level, the air pressure, \(P\), in inches of mercury, is given by the function \[ P = 30 e^{-0.0000323 h}. \] Compute \(dP/dh\) and explain what this derivative function tells you about air pressure, including a discussion of the units on \(dP/dh\). In addition, determine how fast the air pressure is changing for a pilot of a small plane passing through an altitude of \(1000\) feet.
