Item 2
For \(\ds \int_{-1}^4 (2-2x) \, dx\), we first sketch the region bounded by the function, as shown below. The line creates two triangles, one with area \(A_1 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 \cdot 4 = 4\) and the other with area \(A_2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3 \cdot 6 = 9\). Since the latter area corresponds to a region below the \(x\)-axis, we associate a negative sign to it, and hence find that \[ \int_{-1}^4 (2-2x) \, dx = A_1 - A_2 = 4 - 9 = -5. \]
