Solution 8.6.3.2
  1. Note that \[ f'(x) = - 2x + \frac{x^3}{3!} - \frac{x^5}{5!} + \cdots + (-1)^k \frac{x^{k-1}}{(k-1)!} + \cdots . \]
  2. We recognize \(f(x)\) as \(\cos(x)\) and \(f'(x)\) as \(-\sin(x)\). this gives the known differentiation formula \[ \frac{d}{dx} \cos(x) = -\sin(x). \]
