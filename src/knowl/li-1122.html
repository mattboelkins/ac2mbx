Item 2
After differentiating with respect to \(x\), we have \[ \cos(y) \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 + 1. \] Taking the usual steps to solve for \(\frac{dy}{dx}\), we find that \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{3x^2 + 1}{\cos(y) + 1}. \] Evaluating the slope of the tangent line at \((0,0)\), we have \(\left. \frac{dy}{dx} \right|_{(0,0)} = \frac{1}{2},\) and thus the tangent line at \((0,0)\) has equation \(y = \frac{1}{2}x\).
