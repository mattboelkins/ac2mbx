Similar to our work with the first derivative, we observe that \(abe^{-bx}\) is always positive, and thus the sign of \(g''\) depends on the sign of \((bx-2)\), which is zero when \(x = \frac{2}{b}\). Since \((bx-2)\) represents a line with positive slope \((b)\), the value of \((bx-2)\) is negative for \(x \lt \frac{2}{b}\) and positive for \(x > \frac{2}{b}\), and thus the sign chart for \(g''\) is given by the one shown in FigureÂ 3.2.4. Thus, \(g\) is concave down for all \(x \lt \frac{2}{b}\) and concave up for all \(x > \frac{2}{b}\).
