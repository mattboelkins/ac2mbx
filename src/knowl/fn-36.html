Footnote 5.4.1Observe that if we considered the alternate choice, and let \(u = \cos(x)\) and \(dv = x \, dx\), then \(du = -\sin(x) \, dx\) and \(v = \frac{1}{2}x^2\), from which we would write \[ \int x\cos(x) \, dx = \frac{1}{2}x^2 \cos(x) - \int \frac{1}{2}x^2 (-\sin(x)) \, dx. \] Thus we have replaced the problem of integrating \(x \cos(x)\) with that of integrating \(\frac{1}{2}x^2 \sin(x)\); the latter is clearly more complicated, which shows that this alternate choice is not as helpful as the first choice.in-context
