Item 4
Series that involve factorials are good candidates for the Ratio Test: \begin{align*} \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{\frac{100^{k+1}}{(k+1)!}}{\frac{100^k}{k!}} \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{100^{k+1}k!}{100^k(k+1)!}\\ \amp = \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{100}{k+1}\\ \amp = 0. \end{align*} Since this limit is 0, the Ratio Test tells us that the series \(\ds \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \ \frac{100^k}{k!}\) converges.
