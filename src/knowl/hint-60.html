Hint 2.3.3.2
  1. When applying the quotient rule, use parentheses around the bottom function, \(z^4 + 1\), to ensure that when you compute “the bottom times the derivative of the top” that the rule is applied correctly.
  2. When applying the quotient rule, use parentheses around the bottom function, \(\cos(t) + t^2\), and its derivative to ensure that the rule is applied correctly.
  3. Remember that the derivative evaluated at a particular value gives the slope of the tangent line there.
  4. Let the top function be \(100t\) and simply use the constant multiple rule to find its derivative. Further, note that since the units on \(I\) are candles and those on \(t\) are milliseconds, the units on \(I'(t)\) will be candles per millisecond.
