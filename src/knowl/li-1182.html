Here, the numerator tends to \(\infty\) while the denominator tends to \(0^-\). Note well that this limit is not indeterminate, but rather produces a collection of fractions with large positive numerators and small negative denominators. Hence \[ \ds \lim_{x \to \frac{\pi}{2}^-} \frac{\tan(x)}{x-\frac{\pi}{2}} = -\infty. \] In particular, we observe that L'Hopital's Rule is not applicable here.
