In SectionÂ 5.3, we learned the technique of \(u\)-substitution for evaluating indefinite integrals that involve certain composite functions. For example, the indefinite integral \(\int x^3 \sin(x^4) \, dx\) is perfectly suited to \(u\)-substitution, since not only is there a composite function present, but also the inner function's derivative (up to a constant) is multiplying the composite function. Through \(u\)-substitution, we learned a general situation where recognizing the algebraic structure of a function can enable us to find its antiderivative.
