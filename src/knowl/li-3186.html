Item 2
We use the Ratio Test with \(a_k = k|x|^k\): \begin{align*} \lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{ (k+1)|x|^{k+1} }{ k|x|^k } \amp = |x|\lim_{k \to \infty} \frac{k+1}{k}\\ \amp = |x|. \end{align*} So the power series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} kx^k\) converges absolutely when \(|x| \lt 1\) or when \(-1 \lt x \lt 1\) and diverges outside this interval. To completely determine the interval of convergence, we need to check what happens at the endpoints of this interval.
  • When \(x=-1\) our power series is \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} (-1)^k k\). Since \(k \to \infty\) as \(k \to infty\), this series diverges by the Divergence Test.
  • When \(x=1\) our power series is \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} k\) which again diverges by the Divergence Test.
Therefore, the interval of convergence of the power series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} kx^k\) is \((-1,1)\).
