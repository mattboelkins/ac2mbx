  3. From the limit definition, \begin{align*} g'(\frac{\pi}{2}) \amp = \amp \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{g(\frac{\pi}{2} + h) - g(\frac{\pi}{2})}{h}\\ \amp = \amp \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\cos(\frac{\pi}{2} + h) - \cos(\frac{\pi}{2})}{h}\\ \amp = \amp \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}h)}{h} \end{align*} Because we cannot simplify the fraction \(\frac{\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}+h)}{h}\) any further algebraically, we estimate the value of the limit using small values of \(h\). Doing so, it appears that \(\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\cos(\frac{\pi}{2}+h)}{h} = -1\), and thus \(g'(\frac{\pi}{2}) = -1\). This matches the estimate generated visually by sketching the tangent line at \((\frac{\pi}{2},g(\frac{\pi}{2}))\). Finally, by the periodicity of the sine function, we expect the value of the derivative at \(\frac{\pi}{2}\) to match the derivative value at \(-\frac{3\pi}{2}\).
