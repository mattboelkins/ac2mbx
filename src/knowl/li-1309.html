Item 3
As \(x \to \infty\), \(e^{-bx} \to 0\). Thus, \[ \lim_{x \to \infty} a(1-e^{-bx}) = \lim_{x \to \infty} a - ae^{-bx} = a - 0 = a. \] This shows that \(h\) has a horizontal asymptote at \(y = a\) as we move rightward on its graph. As \(x \to -\infty\), \(e^{-bx} \to \infty\). Thus, \[ \lim_{x \to \infty} a(1-e^{-bx}) = \lim_{x \to \infty} a - ae^{-bx} = -\infty. \]
