Item 3
As \(x \to 0^+\), \(\ln(x) \to -\infty\) and \(\frac{1}{x} \to +\infty\), thus by L'Hopital's Rule, \[ \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\ln(x)}{\frac{1}{x}} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\frac{1}{x}}{-\frac{1}{x^2}}. \] Reciprocating, multiplying, and simplifying, it follows that \[ \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{\ln(x)}{\frac{1}{x}} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{1}{x}\cdot \frac{x^2}{-1} = \lim_{x \to 0^+} -x = 0. \]
