When we find an antiderivative, we will often say that we evaluate an indefinite integral\knownindex{\lt main>indefinite integral\lt /main>\lt sub>evaluate\lt /sub>}; said differently, the instruction to evaluate an indefinite integral means to find the general antiderivative. Just as the notation \(\frac{d}{dx} [ \Box ]\) means “find the derivative with respect to \(x\) of \(\Box\),” the notation \(\int \Box \, dx\) means “find a function of \(x\) whose derivative is \(\Box\).”
