Item 3
For \(\ds \int_{-1}^1 \sqrt{1-x^2} \, dx\), we simply observe that this function is the top half of a circle of radius 1, and thus the bounded region is a semicircle of radius 1, thus having an area of \(\frac{\pi}{2}\). Therefore, \[ \ds \int_{-1}^1 \sqrt{1-x^2} \, dx = \frac{\pi}{2}. \]
