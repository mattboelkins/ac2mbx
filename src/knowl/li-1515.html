Item 5
It is given in the problem setting that water is entering the tank at a rate of 4 cubic feet per minute, hence \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\), and we are interested in the rate of change of the water's depth when \(h = 3\). Substituting these values into the equation that relates \(\frac{dV}{dt}\) and \(\frac{dh}{dt}\), we find that \[ 4 = \frac{1}{16} \pi 3^2 \left. \frac{dh}{dt} \right|_{h=3}, \] so that \(\left. \frac{dh}{dt} \right|_{h=3} = \frac{64}{9\pi} \approx 2.2635\) feet per minute.
