Item 1
Since the units on a rectangle in a Riemann sum are cal/min for the height and min for the width, the units on the area of such a rectangle are calories, and hence the units on area under the curve \(y = c(t)\) are given in total calories. Hence, the total calories burned during the first 10 minutes of the workout is given by the definite integral \(\int_0^{10} c(t) \, dt\). We use the FTC and evaluate the integral, finding that \begin{align*} \int_0^{10} (-0.05t^2 + t + 10) \, dt \amp = \amp \left. \left( -\frac{0.05}{3} t^3 + \frac{1}{2} t^2 + 10t \right) \right|_0^{10}\\ \amp = \amp \left( -\frac{0.05}{3} (10)^3 + \frac{1}{2} (10)^2 + 10(10) \right) - (-0 + 0 + 0)\\ \amp = \amp \frac{400}{3} \ \mbox{calories} . \end{align*} Thus, the person burned approximately 133.33 calories in the first 10 minutes of the workout.
