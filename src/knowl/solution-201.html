Solution 8.6.3.1
  1. Recall that \[ g(x) = \frac{1}{1-x} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} x^k \] for \(-1 \lt x \lt 1\).
  2. Substituting \(-x^2\) for \(x\) in the power series expansion for \(g(x)\) gives \begin{align*} f(x^2) \amp = g(-x^2)\\ \amp = \frac{1}{1-(-x^2)}\\ \amp = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \left(-x^2\right)^k\\ \amp = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^k x^{2k}. \end{align*} ]
  3. This power series expansion for \(f(x)\) will be valid as long as \(-1 \lt (-x)^2 \lt 1\) or for \(-1 \lt x \lt 1\).
