Item 1
Since the derivatives of \(\cos(x)\) are all either \(\pm \cos(x)\) or \(\pm \sin(x)\), we see that all of the derivatives of \(\cos(x)\) are bounded above by 1. If \(P_n(x)\) is the \(n\)th order Taylor polynomial for \(\cos(x)\), then for any \(x\) we have \[ \left|P_n(x) - f(x)\right| \leq (1)\frac{|x|^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}. \] Since \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{|x|^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} = 0, \] we conclude that \(P_n(x) \to f(x)\) as \(n \to \infty\) for all \(x\) and the Taylor series for \(\cos(x)\) converges to \(\cos(x)\) for every real number \(x\).
