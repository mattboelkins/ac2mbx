Item 4
We can evaluate the improper integral as follows: \begin{align*} \int_{1}^{\infty} f(x) \ dx \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \int_{1}^{t} \frac{1}{x} \ dx\\ \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \ln(x) |_{1}^{t}\\ \amp = \lim_{t \to \infty} \left(\ln(t) - \ln(1) \right)\\ \amp = \infty. \end{align*} Since the value of the series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k}\) exceeds the value of the infinite improper integral, we must conclude that the series \(\ds \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k}\) diverges.
