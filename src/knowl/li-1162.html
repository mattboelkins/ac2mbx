Item 2
The given graph tells us that \(p(2) = 1.5\), \(p'(2)=-1\), \(q(2)=1.5\), and \(q'(2)=0\). Note well that the given limit, \[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}, \] is not indeterminate, and thus L'Hopital's Rule does not apply. Rather, since \(p(x) \to 1.5\) and \(q(x) \to 1.5\) as \(x \to 2\), we have that \[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} = \frac{p(2)}{q(2)} = \frac{1.5}{1.5} = 1. \]
