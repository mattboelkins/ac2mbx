Item 1
Find the tangent line to \(f\) at \(x=0\) and use this linearization to approximate \(e\). That is, find a formula \(L(x)\) for the tangent line, and compute \(L(1)\), since \(L(1) \approx f(1) = e\). Solution

The linearization of \(f\) at \(x=a\) is

\[ f(a) + f'(a)(x-a), \]

so the linearization \(P_1(x)\) of \(f(x) = e^x\) at \(x=0\) is

\[ P_1(x) = e^0 + e^0(x-0) = 1+x. \]

Now

\[ f(x) \approx P_1(x) \]

for \(x\) close to \(0\) and so

\[ e = e^1 \approx P_1(1) = 1+1 = 2. \]
