It makes sense intuitively that these two quantities are involved in understanding the rate of change of a composite function: if we are considering \(C(x) = f(g(x))\) and asking how fast \(C\) is changing at a given \(x\) value as \(x\) changes, it clearly matters (a) how fast \(g\) is changing at \(x\), as well as how fast \(f\) is changing at the value of \(g(x)\).
