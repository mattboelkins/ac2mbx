\item The method of partial fractions enables any rational function to be antidifferentiated, because any polynomial function can be factored into a product of linear and irreducible quadratic terms. This allows any rational function to be written as the sum of a polynomial plus rational terms of the form \(\frac{A}{(x-c)^n}\) (where \(n\) is a natural number) and \(\frac{Bx+C}{x^2 + k}\) (where \(k\) is a positive real number). \item Until the development of computing algebra systems, integral tables enabled students of calculus to more easily evaluate integrals such as \(\int \sqrt{a^2 + u^2} \, du\), where \(a\) is a positive real number.
