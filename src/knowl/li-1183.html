Item 5
In its original form, \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} xe^{-x}\), is indeterminate of form \(\infty \cdot 0\). Rewriting \(e^{-x}\) as \(\frac{1}{e^x}\), a straightforward application of L'Hopital's Rule tells us that \[ \lim_{x \to \infty} xe^{-x} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x}{e^x} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{e^x}. \] Since \(e^x \to \infty\) as \(x \to \infty\), we find that \[ \lim_{x \to \infty} xe^{-x} = 0. \]
