Hint 2.7.2.4
  1. Note that the numerator of \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) is a quadratic function of \(x\). What are its zeros?
  2. The denominator of \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) is a cubic function of \(y\). Use appropriate technology to determine the zeros of this function.
  3. When \(x = 1\), then \(y\) must satisfy the equation \(y(y^2-1)(y-2) = 0\). Evaluate \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) at one such point and use your result appropriately.
