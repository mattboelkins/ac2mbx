Hint 3.2.2.2
  1. Note that \(p'(x) = 3x^2 - a\). When solving \(p'(x) = 0\), think about two possible cases: when \(a > 0\) and when \(a \lt 0\).
  2. Remember that any quadratic function can be zero at most two times. How does the graph of \(y = 3x^2 - a\) look?
  3. Don't forget that \(\frac{d}{dx}[a] = 0\).
  4. Think about how a typical cubic polynomial's graph behaves and use your work in (a) - (c).
