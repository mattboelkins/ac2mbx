Next we examine what happens when we take a sum of two functions. If we have \(y = f(x)\) and \(y = g(x)\), we can compute a new function \(y = (f+g)(x)\) by adding the outputs of the two functions: \((f+g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)\). Not only does this result in the value of the new function being the sum of the values of the two known functions, but also the slope of the new function is the sum of the slopes of the known functions.
