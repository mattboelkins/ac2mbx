Item 2
Now apply the Ratio Test to determine if the series \(\sum \frac{b^k}{k!}\) converges for any constant \(b\). Solution

\solution We use the Ratio Test with \(a_n = \frac{b^n}{n!}\). First we consider the ratios of successive terms:

\begin{align*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \amp = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{ \frac{b^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} }{ \frac{b^{n}}{(n)!} }\\ \amp = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{b}{n+1}\\ \amp = 0. \end{align*}

Since the limit of the ratio of successive terms is less than 1, we conclude that the series \(\sum \frac{b^k}{k!}\) converges.
