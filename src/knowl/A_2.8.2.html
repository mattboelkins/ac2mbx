Exercise 2.8.4

In this activity, we reason graphically from the following figure to evaluate limits of ratios of functions about which some information is known.

  1. Use the left-hand graph to determine the values of \(f(2)\), \(f'(2)\), \(g(2)\), and \(g'(2)\). Then, evaluate \[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}. \]
  2. Use the middle graph to find \(p(2)\), \(p'(2)\), \(q(2)\), and \(q'(2)\). Then, determine the value of \[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}. \]
  3. Use the right-hand graph to compute \(r(2)\), \(r'(2)\), \(s(2)\), \(s'(2)\). Explain why you cannot determine the exact value of \[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{r(x)}{s(x)} \] without further information being provided, but that you can determine the sign of \(\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{r(x)}{s(x)}\). In addition, state what the sign of the limit will be, with justification.
