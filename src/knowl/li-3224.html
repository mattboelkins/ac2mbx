Item 2
Substitute your results from part (a) into the Airy equation and show that we can write Equation (8.6.4) in the form \begin{equation} \sum_{k=2}^{\infty} (k-1)ka_kx^{k-2} + \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_kx^{k+1} = 0. \label{eq_PA8.6_Airy_1}\tag{8.6.5}\end{equation}Solution

We substitute our series from (a) into the Airy equation to obtain the equation

\[ \sum_{k=2}^{\infty} (k-1)ka_kx^{k-2} + x\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} a_kx^{k} = 0. \]

Distributing the \(x\) in the second term on the left yields the equation in (8.6.5).
