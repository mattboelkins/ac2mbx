Item 1
By the chain rule and treating \(A\), \(c\), and \(k\) as constants, we find that \[ L'(t) = A(-1)(1+ce^{-kt})^{-2} ce^{-kt}(-k) = Acke^{-kt}(1+ce^{-kt})^{-2}. \] Since \(A\), \(c\), and \(k\) are all positive and \(e^{-kt} > 0\) for all values of \(t\), it is apparent that \(L'(t)\) is never zero, and indeed is positive for every value of \(t\). Thus, \(L\) is an always increasing function.
