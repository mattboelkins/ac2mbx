Hint 2.4.2.4
  1. Remember that one value of \(x\) for which \(\sin(x) = 0\) is \(x = \pi\). What are the others?
  2. Don't forget that \(\frac{d}{dx}[1] = 0\), and be careful with your signs.
  3. Consider rewriting \(\frac{\cos(x)}{\sin^2(x)}\) as \(\frac{1}{\sin(x)} \cdot \frac{\cos(x)}{\sin(x)}\).
  4. Observe that \(\sin(x)\) is still present in the denominator of \(h'(x)\).
