Solution.     We know that \(f'(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(2+h) - f(2)}{h}\). But since we don't have a graph for \(y = f(x)\) nor a formula for the function, we can neither sketch a tangent line nor evaluate the limit exactly. We can't even use smaller and smaller values of \(h\) to estimate the limit. Instead, we have just two choices: using \(h = -1\) or \(h = 1\), depending on which point we pair with \((2,3.25)\).
