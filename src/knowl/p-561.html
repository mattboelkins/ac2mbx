In FigureÂ 2.6.3, we saw an interesting relationship between the slopes of tangent lines to the natural exponential and natural logarithm functions at points that corresponded to reflection across the line \(y = x\). In particular, we observed that for a point such as \((\ln(2), 2)\) on the graph of \(f(x) = e^x\), the slope of the tangent line at this point is \(f'(\ln(2)) = 2\), while at the corresponding point \((2, \ln(2))\) on the graph of \(f^{-1}(x) = \ln(x)\), the slope of the tangent line at this point is \((f^{-1})'(2) = \frac{1}{2}\), which is the reciprocal of \(f'(\ln(2))\).
