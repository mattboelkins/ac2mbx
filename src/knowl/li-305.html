Item 3
If \(C(2000) = 800\) and \(C'(2000) = 0.35\), then we know once 2000 feet of rope are produced, the total cost function is increasing at $0.35 per additional foot of rope. Then, if we manufacture an additional 100 feet of rope, the additional total cost will be approximately \[ 100 \ \mbox{feet} \cdot 0.35 \ \frac{\mbox{dollars} }{\mbox{foot} } = 35 \ \mbox{dollars} . \] Therefore, we find that \(C(2100) \approx C(2000) + 35 = 835,\) or that the cost to make 2100 feet of rope is about $835.
