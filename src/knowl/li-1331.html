Item 4
From (b), we know that \(t = -\frac{1}{k} \ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right)\) is the location of the inflection point of \(L\). Evaluating the function at this point, we find that \[ L\left( -\frac{1}{k} \ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right) \right) = \frac{A}{1+ce^{-k [-\frac{1}{k} \ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right)]}} = \frac{A}{1+ce^{\ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right)}} = \frac{A}{1+c \cdot \frac{1}{c}} = \frac{A}{2}. \] Thus, the inflection point on the graph of \(L\) is located at \(( -\frac{1}{k} \ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right), \frac{A}{2})\).
