Item 4
The particle's instantaneous velocity at the moment \(t = 1\) is given by \(s'(1)\). We use the quotient rule to find \(s'(t)\), and simplify by removing a common factor of \(e^t\) to get \[ s'(t) = \frac{e^t(-3\sin(t) - \cos(t))-(3\cos(t) - \sin(t))e^t}{(e^t)^2} = \frac{-2\sin(t)-4\cos(t)}{e^t}. \] Thus, \(s'(1) = \frac{-2\sin(1)-4\cos(1)}{e^1} \approx -1.414\), which is the particle's instantaneous velocity in feet per second at the moment \(t = 1\).
