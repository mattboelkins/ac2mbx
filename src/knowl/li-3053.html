Item 10
For this example we will use the Integral Test with the substitution \(w = \ln(x)\), \(dw = \frac{1}{x} \ dx\): \begin{align*} \displaystyle \int_2^{\infty} \frac{1}{x \ln(x)} \ dx \amp = \lim_{b \to \infty} \int_2^b \frac{1}{x \ln(x)} \ dx\\ \amp = \displaystyle \lim_{b \to \infty} \int_{\ln(2)}^{\ln(b)} \frac{1}{w} \ dw\\ \amp = \displaystyle \lim_{b \to \infty} \ln(w) \bigm|_{\ln(2)}^{\ln(b)}\\ \amp = \displaystyle \lim_{b \to \infty} \left[ \ln(\ln(b)) - \ln(\ln(2)) \right]\\ \amp = \infty. \end{align*} Since \(\ds \int_2^{\infty} \frac{1}{x \ln(x)} \ dx\) diverges, we conclude \(\ds \sum_{k=2}^{\infty} \ \frac{1}{k \ln(k)}\) diverges.
