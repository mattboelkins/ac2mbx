Note that while we read \(\frac{\triangle y}{\triangle x}\) as “change in \(y\) over change in \(x\),” for the derivative symbol \(\frac{dy}{dx}\), we view this is a single symbol, not a quotient of two quantities 1 That is, we do not say “dee-y over dee-x.”. For example, if \(y = x^2\), we'll write that the derivative is \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x.\)
