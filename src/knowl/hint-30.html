Hint 1.6.4.2
  1. Remember that a function is increasing on an interval if and only if its first derivative is positive on the interval and that \(v(t) = s'(t)\).
  2. See (a), and note that \(v'(t) = s''(t)\).
  3. Remember that the first derivative of a function measures its instantaneous rate of change, so \(s''(t)\) measures the instantaneous rate of change of \(v(t) = s'(t)\).
  4. Note that \(s''(t) = [s'(t)]'\), so \(s''(t)\) is the slope of the tangent line to \(y = s'(t)\).
  5. Be very careful with your letters: \(s\), \(v\), and \(a\). For instance, note that when acceleration is positive, velocity must be increasing.
