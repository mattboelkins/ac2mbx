Item 3
Just like we can find the average velocity of a moving body by computing change in position over change in time, we can compute the average rate of change of any function \(f\). In particular, the average rate of change of a function \(f\) over an interval \([a,b]\) is the quotient \[ \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}. \] What does the quantity \(\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\) measure on the graph of \(y = f(x)\) over the interval \([a,b]\)?
