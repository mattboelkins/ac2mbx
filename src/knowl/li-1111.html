Item 3
There are four points on the curve where \(x = 1\), which correspond to the \(y\)-values that satisfy \(y(y^2-1)(y-2) = 0\): \((1,0)\), \((1,1)\), \((1,-1)\), \((1,2)\). We choose the point \((1,1)\) and evaluate \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) at this point. Doing so, \[ \left.\frac{dy}{dx} \right|_{(1,1)} = \frac{(1-1)(1-2) + 1(1-2) + 1(1-1)}{(1^2-1)(1-2) + 2\cdot 1^2(1-2) + 1(1^2-1)} = \frac{-1}{-2} = \frac{1}{2}. \] Thus, the equation of the tangent line to the curve at \((1,1)\) is \(y - 1 = \frac{1}{2}(x-1)\).
