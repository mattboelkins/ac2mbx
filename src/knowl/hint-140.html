Hint 4.3.2.2
  1. Sketch the region bounded by \(y = 3x\) and the \(x\)-axis on \([0,1]\) and observe that it forms a familiar geometric shape.
  2. Sketch the region bounded by \(y = 2-2x\) and the \(x\)-axis on \([-1,4]\); think carefully about the role of signed area in determining the value of the integral, and subdivide the problem by considering regions on which the curve is positive and negative.
  3. Observe that \(y = \sqrt{1-x^2}\) is the top half the circle whose equation is \(x^2 + y^2 = 1\).
  4. Use known formulas for the area of a triangle, square, or circle appropriately. Keep in mind the role of signed area when dealing with the portions of the region that lie below the \(x\)-axis.
