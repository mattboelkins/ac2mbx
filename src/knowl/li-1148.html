Item 1
As \(x \to 0\), we see that \(\ln(1+x) \to \ln(1) = 0\), thus this limit has an indeterminate form. By L'Hopital's Rule, we have \[ \ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\ln(1 + x)}{x} = \ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{1}{1 + x}}{1}. \] As this limit is no longer indeterminate, we may simply allow \(x \to 0\), and thus we find that \[ \ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\ln(1 + x)}{x} = \frac{\frac{1}{1 + 0}}{1} = 1. \]
