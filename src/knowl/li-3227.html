Item 5
We can now substitute (8.6.6) and (8.6.7) into (8.6.5) to obtain \begin{equation} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (n+1)(n+2)a_{n+2}x^{n} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_{n-1}x^{n} = 0. \label{eq_PA8.6_Airy_2}\tag{8.6.8}\end{equation} Combine the like powers of \(x\) in the two series to show that our solution must satisfy \begin{equation} 2a_2 + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \left[(k+1)(k+2)a_{k+2}+a_{k-1} \right] x^{k} = 0. \label{eq_PA8.6_Airy_sum_3}\tag{8.6.9}\end{equation}Solution

Notice that we have like powers of \(x\) in our two series, so we can combine them and obtain

\begin{align*} 0 \amp = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (k+1)(k+2)a_{k+2}x^{k} + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_{k-1}x^{k}\\ \amp = \left[(1)(2)a_2 + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} (k+1)(k+2)a_{k+2}x^{k} \right] + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} a_{k-1}x^{k}\\ \amp = 2a_2 + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \left[(k+1)(k+2)a_{k+2}+a_{k-1} \right] x^{k}. \end{align*}
