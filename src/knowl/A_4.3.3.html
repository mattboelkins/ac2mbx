Exercise 4.3.14

Suppose that \(v(t) = \sqrt{4-(t-2)^2}\) tells us the instantaneous velocity of a moving object on the interval \(0 \le t \le 4\), where \(t\) is measured in minutes and \(v\) is measured in meters per minute.

  1. Sketch an accurate graph of \(y = v(t)\). What kind of curve is \(y = \sqrt{4-(t-2)^2}\)?
  2. Evaluate \(\int_0^4 v(t) \, dt\) exactly.
  3. In terms of the physical problem of the moving object with velocity \(v(t)\), what is the meaning of \(\int_0^4 v(t) \, dt\)? Include units on your answer.
  4. Determine the exact average value of \(v(t)\) on \([0,4]\). Include units on your answer.
  5. Sketch a rectangle whose base is the line segment from \(t=0\) to \(t = 4\) on the \(t\)-axis such that the rectangle's area is equal to the value of \(\int_0^4 v(t) \, dt\). What is the rectangle's exact height?
  6. How can you use the average value you found in (d) to compute the total distance traveled by the moving object over \([0,4]\)?
