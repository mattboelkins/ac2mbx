Hint 3.2.2.4
  1. Expand to write \(h(x) = a - ae^{-bx}\) before differentiating, and remember to treat \(a\) and \(b\) as constants.
  2. Remember that \(e^{-bx}\) is never zero and always positive, regardless of the value of \(x\), and that \(a\) and \(b\) are positive constants.
  3. Recall that \(e^{-x} \to 0\) as \(x \to \infty\) and \(e^{-x} \to \infty\) as \(x \to -\infty\).
  4. Consider how \(b\) affects the value of \(h'(x)\).
  5. Use your work in (a)-(d).
