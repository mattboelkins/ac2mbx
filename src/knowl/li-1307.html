Item 1
Since \(h(x) = a - ae^{-bx}\), we have by the constant multiple and chain rules that \[ h'(x) = -ae^{-bx}(-b) = abe^{-bx}. \] Since \(a\) and \(b\) are positive constants and \(e^{-bx} > 0\) for all \(x\), we see that \(h'(x)\) is never zero (nor undefined), and indeed \(h'(x) > 0\) for all \(x\). Hence \(h\) is an always increasing function.
