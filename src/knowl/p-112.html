\item The average rate of change of a function \(f\) on the interval \([a,b]\) is \(\ds \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\). The units on the average rate of change are units of \(f\) per unit of \(x\), and the numerical value of the average rate of change represents the slope of the secant line between the points \((a,f(a))\) and \((b,f(b))\) on the graph of \(y = f(x)\).
