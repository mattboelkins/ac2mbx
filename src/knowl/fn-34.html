Footnote 5.3.2If we recall from the definition of the derivative that \(\frac{du}{dx} \approx \frac{\triangle{u}}{\triangle{x}}\) and use the fact that \(\frac{du}{dx} = g'(x)\), then we see that \(g'(x) \approx \frac{\triangle{u}}{\triangle{x}}\). Solving for \(\triangle u\), \(\triangle u \approx g'(x) \triangle x\). It is this last relationship that, when expressed in “differential” notation enables us to write \(du = g'(x) \, dx\) in the change of variable formula.in-context
