Item 1
Assume that we can differentiate a power series term by term, just like we can differentiate a (finite) polynomial. Use the fact that \[ f(x) = 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \frac{x^6}{6!} + \cdots + (-1)^k \frac{x^{2k}}{(2k)!} + \cdots \] to find a power series expansion for \(f'(x)\).
