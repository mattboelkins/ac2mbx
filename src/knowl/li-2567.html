Item 1
We will create a new approximation by assuming the error is exactly proportional to \(\Delta t\), according to the formula \[ y(\overline{t})-E_{\Delta t} =K\Delta t. \] Using our earlier results from the initial value problem \(dy/dt = y\) and \(y(0)=1\) with \(\Delta t = 0.2\) and \(\Delta t = 0.1\), we have \begin{align*} y(1) - 2.4883 \amp = \amp 0.2K\\ y(1) - 2.5937 \amp = \amp 0.1K. \end{align*} This is a system of two linear equations in the unknowns \(y(1)\) and \(K\). Solve this system to find a new approximation for \(y(1)\). (You may remember that the exact value is \(y(1) = e = 2.71828\ldots.\))
