Solution .1

In this case we know that \(k^3 \lt k^3+1\) and so if \(k > 0\) then \(\frac{1}{k^3} > \frac{1}{k^3+1}\). It follows that \(\frac{k}{k^3} > \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) and so the series \( \sum \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) is bounded below by 0 and above by the series \( \sum \frac{k}{k^3}\). Now

\[ \frac{k}{k^3} = \frac{1}{k^2} \]

and so the series \( \sum \frac{k}{k^3}\) is a \(p\)-series with \(p=2\) and converges. The Direct Comparison Test shows that the smaller series \( \sum \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) must also converge.
